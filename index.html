Deep learning models for dermoscopic skin lesion hair segmentation: An extensive experimental study.
The study aims to address the challenges of automatic melanoma diagnosis by improving hair segmentation from skin lesions using deep learning (DL) models. This process is crucial for enhancing the accuracy of melanoma detection, as hair artifacts can obscure lesion areas. The research evaluates ten DL methods for hair segmentation, trained with different weight configurations and encoder backbones on a public dermoscopic dataset. The performance of these models is assessed using various metrics and computational complexity analyses. Additionally, statistical significance tests validate the differences in model performance, ensuring that improvements are not due to random variations. The findings reveal that the pretrained UNet++ model with ResNet-50 encoder is the most accurate for hair segmentation. For low-resource environments, pretrained PSPNet or FPN with ResNet-18 encoder are recommended, while pretrained UNet++ or LinkNet with ResNet-18 encoder offer a balance between performance and computational efficiency.
Adaptive BERT-CNN Model for Efficient Maintenance of Variable Length Reviews in Rating Prediction.
The study introduces the Adaptive BERT-CNN model to improve the analysis of long text reviews for recommender systems. Traditional models like BERT struggle with long texts, so the proposed model combines BERT with CNN using a chunking technique that segments long reviews into smaller parts while preserving context. This allows CNNs to capture patterns across these chunks, enhancing sentiment analysis and recommendations. The model was tested on various datasets and achieved a high accuracy of 99.76%, significantly outperforming baseline models like BERT and CNN, demonstrating its effectiveness in handling long reviews and understanding user sentiments.

Exploring Transformer and Recurrent Neural Models for Sentiment Analysis of Mobile App Reviews. [to appear]
Georgr Tonmoy Roy, and Dipannita Biswas.
In 2024 2nd International Conference on Information and Communication Technology (ICICT2024).
Sentiment analysis is one of the important aspects of discovering user opinions and enhancing the quality of products like mobile applications. This study assesses the effectiveness of six prominent Natural Language Processing (NLP) models—BERT, RoBERTa, XLNet, Recurrent Neural Network (RNN), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), and Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU)—in classifying sentiments from a large dataset of Google Play Store reviews. We collected 73,483 reviews from 45 apps, preprocessed the data, and employed two labeling methods: a pre-trained DistilBERT model and K-means clustering. Our results compare transformer-based models, BERT and XLNet, with traditional recurrent models for sentiment analysis of mobile application reviews. The results show that transformer-based models achieve higher accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 scores with DistilBERT pre-trained model labeling. However, no single model strikes a balance between recall and precision using K-means clustering labels. These findings suggest that the choice of labeling method, in addition to the use of advanced transformer models and embeddings, significantly affects model performance.

Detection of Schizophrenia Using Attention Mechanism with Convolutional Neural Network on EEG Signal. [to appear]
Mohammad Khairul Alam Bhuiyan, and Dipannita Biswas.
In 2024 IEEE International Conference on Power, Electrical, Electronics, and Industrial Applications.
Schizophrenia (SZ) is a challenging mental disorder, requiring accurate diagnosis for effective treatment. Early diagnosis of schizophrenia can facilitate a normal life for patients. Electroencephalogram (EEG) data contains valuable insights into brain network connectivity, enabling the detection of abnormalities associated with schizophrenia. Hence, this study proposes a methodology employing a deep learning-based model utilizing deep convolutional neural networks(CNNs) with an Attention mechanism to classify schizophrenia patients. In this research, each EEG signal undergoes decomposition with Continuous Wavelet Transform (CWT) to extract relevant features that capture the underlying neurophysiological characteristics of brain activity. These features are then utilized to distinguish schizophrenia patients from healthy individuals using the proposed method, which is evaluated on an EEG dataset comprising 45 schizophrenic patients and 39 healthy subjects. To provide a comparison, various deep learning models like ResNet-50,CNN and DenseNet Model are applied. The results indicate that the proposed model outperforms all other methods tested.

MFID: An Improved Matrix Factorization Model for Incremental Data. paper
Dipannita Biswas, and Dr. K.M. Azharul Hasan.
In 2023 26th International Conference on Computer and Information Technology (ICCIT).
Matrix factorization, a widely used technique in various data analysis domains, is extended to address the challenges of incremental data updates while maintaining computational efficiency. Our novel approach, termed Matrix Factorization for Incremental Data (MFID), focuses on updating a single factor matrix, either rows or columns, in response to the incremental addition of data. This innovative adaptation significantly reduces computational overhead compared to traditional methods that require full re-factorization of the matrix.

Progressive Recommendation by Incremental Tensor Factorization. paper
Dipannita Biswas, Dr. K.M. Azharul Hasan, and Zaima Zarnaz.
In 2022 25th International Conference on Computer and Information Technology (ICCIT).
There are several circumstances in which constantly updated multidimensional tensor data must be analyzed in real-time in order to yield quick recommendation in our fast-changing data world. Methods for incremental tensor decompositions are powerful tools for analyzing and predicting fast-growing multidimensional datasets. In this research, we provide a robust model that overcomes the limitations of the most popular incremental tensor decomposition methods and yields high-accuracy prediction results for enormous datasets with fast execution time. Testing our model on datasets, we discovered that it was able to create a tensor summary that could reflect both the new and old datasets properly and performed better than traditional static methods.

Credit Card Approval Prediction by Non-negative Tensor Factorization. paper
Zaima Zarnaz, Dipannita Biswas, and Dr. K.M. Azharul Hasan.
In International Conference on Robotics, Electrical and Signal Processing Techniques (ICREST).
With the increasing number of credit card applications, banks are opting towards the use of prediction-based algorithms as opposed to manual approval methods. Data analysis has exhibited a strong correlation between several financial and personal factors of a client and the likelihood of said client complying with their respective bank's credit policies. In this paper, we propose the use of the PARAFAC tensor factorization method to predict and grant credit cards to applicants based on the customers' activity history. We used six financial and personal factors and constructed a tensor by reducing them into three factors. We predicted the resulting factors through the use of alternating least squares algorithm with an emphasis on error minimization and finally re-constructed the original tensor.

    (2k19)
